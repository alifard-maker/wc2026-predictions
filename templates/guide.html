1. The basics

  • You join a private pool with friends — grab the invite link at the bottom of this Guide.
  • For each World Cup match, predict the full-time score (home goals – away goals).
  • Enter picks on Matches, tap Save Predictions, and check the Leaderboard as results come in.
  • All times on this site are Eastern Time (ET).

Install on your phone

This site is a web app — no App Store download required. You can pin it to your home screen so it opens like a normal app (sometimes called a PWA).

iPhone / iPad (Safari)
  1. Open this site in Safari (the built-in browser).
  2. Tap the Share button (square with an arrow pointing up).
  3. Scroll down and tap Add to Home Screen.
  4. Tap Add. An icon appears on your home screen — open the pool from there.

Use Safari for this — other browsers on iOS may not offer Add to Home Screen.

Android (Chrome)
  1. Open this site in Chrome.
  2. Tap the menu (⋮, top right).
  3. Tap Add to Home screen or Install app (wording varies by phone).
  4. Confirm — the icon is added to your home screen or app drawer.

Samsung Internet and other Android browsers have a similar option in their menu. You still need internet for live scores and picks.

This is not a separate App Store app — it’s a shortcut to the same website, full screen, with your pool login saved like any other site. Handy for match days when you want one-tap access.

2. Deadlines

Match predictions lock 1 hour before kickoff.

Example: kickoff Friday 3:00 PM ET → deadline Friday 2:00 PM ET.

  • Each match shows Open until … while you can still change your pick.
  • With 1 hour left before the deadline (2 hours before kickoff), that badge flashes so you don’t miss it.
  • After the deadline the match is Closed — no more edits.
Tournament picks (winner, 2nd, 3rd, top scorer) lock {{ tournament_deadline.strftime('%a %d %b %Y, %H:%M') }} ET — one minute before the opening match (Mexico vs South Africa).

3. Match scoring

Points are added after the final whistle.

Group stage & Round of 32

Predict the full-time score after 90 minutes (group stage) or the final scoreline including extra time if needed (Round of 32). Round of 32 picks must have a winner in the score — no draws.

Round of 16 onward — draws & penalties

From the Round of 16 to the final, knockout ties often go to a penalty shootout. You may predict a draw after extra time (e.g. 1–1). If you do, you must also pick which team wins on penalties.

  • Non-draw pick (e.g. 2–1) — you’re predicting that team wins before or during extra time.
  • Draw pick (e.g. 1–1 + Morocco on pens) — you’re predicting the ET score and who advances after the shootout.
Your pickPoints
Exact score (e.g. you said 2–1, it finished 2–1 after ET)5
Exact ET draw + correct pens winner (e.g. 1–1 + Morocco on pens)5
Correct result (right winner; wrong score — or pens winner when it went to shootout)2
Wrong outcome0

Example: Netherlands 1–1 Morocco (Morocco on pens). A pick of 0–3 Morocco earns 2 pts (correct winner). A pick of 1–1 + Morocco on pens earns 5 pts (exact ET score and pens winner).

Phase prediction bonus (+15)

When an entire phase of the World Cup is complete, the player with the most correct predictions in that phase earns +15 bonus points. A pick counts if you got the exact score or the correct result (win/draw/loss). Tied leaders each get +15.

  • Group stage — all 72 opening matches
  • Round of 32 — 16 knockout matches
  • Round of 16 — 8 matches
  • Quarter-finals — 4 matches
  • Semi-finals — 2 matches
  • Final & 3rd place — the final and the third-place match

Awards appear on the Leaderboard once every match in that phase has a result. Missing picks count as 0 for that match.

4. Bold pick (2× points)

  • Each calendar day (ET) with two or more matches, choose one as your 🎰 Bold 2× pick.
  • Single-match days (many knockout evenings) have no bold — everyone plays the same stakes.
  • From the Round of 32, picks must have a winner in the score — no draws (ET and pens decide ties in real life).
  • From the Round of 16, you may pick a draw after extra time — but you must choose who wins on penalties.
  • If your bold pick scores points, they double: exact 5 → 10, correct result 2 → 4.
  • On Matches: enter scores, tap the 🎰 Bold 2× pill under the match, then Save Predictions.
  • On a match page: use Make this your bold pick (moves your bold if you change your mind).
  • Wrong bold picks still earn 0 — bold only multiplies points you actually win.

5. When picks are revealed

🔒 Hidden until deadline — other players’ scores stay secret until that match’s prediction window closes.
  • Before deadline: you see your own picks on Matches and match pages; everyone else’s (including the pool admin) show as Hidden. The picks ticker only rolls AI agents until deadlines pass.
  • After deadline: all pool picks unlock — compare scores, see consensus, and chat about the matchup before kickoff.
  • Tournament picks from other humans stay hidden until the tournament deadline; AI agent tournament picks stay visible (try to beat the bots).
  • Recaps light up once deadlines pass — hive minds, bold calls, lone wolves — then final-whistle awards after results.

7. Standings & predicted brackets

Open Standings for live group tables and the knockout tree. There are two modes — don’t mix them up:

Actual results

The default view. It follows the real World Cup as scores are entered in the pool.

  • Group stage — tables for Groups A–L with points, goal difference, and who’s in the top two (or in the hunt for a best-third spot).
  • Colour rows — green = qualified or leading the group; yellow = third-place race; grey = out or bottom.
  • Knockout bracket — fills in as real results arrive. Tap a match to open its page. Scroll sideways on mobile for the full tree.
  • Champion banner — appears at the top once the final is decided in real life.

Predicted brackets

Switch to Predicted brackets to ask: “What would the World Cup look like if everyone’s score picks came true?”

  • Uses your pool’s score predictions — not official results — to simulate group tables and knockouts.
  • Group tables are recalculated from predicted scores. Until you’ve picked every match in a group, teams show as leading or contending instead of fully qualified.
  • Who advances — top two from each group plus the best eight third-placed teams (same rules as the real tournament), then a full Round of 32 through to the final.
  • Knockout winners come from your predicted score for each match. From the Round of 16, a draw pick uses your penalty-shootout winner. Earlier rounds (Round of 32) use the scoreline winner only.
  • Matches you’ve predicted show a Pred tag and the score you entered. Unpicked knockouts stay TBD until earlier rounds are filled in.
  • Predicted champion — shown on the trophy at the end of the bracket (or Bracket incomplete if not enough picks yet).

Compare predictors

  • In predicted mode, use View bracket for to flip between pool members (including AI bots).
  • Overview cards show each player’s predicted champion and how many picks they’ve entered — handy for “you have France winning, I have Argentina” debates.
  • The more group and knockout matches someone has predicted, the more complete their simulated bracket.
Important: Predicted brackets are for fun and bragging rights — they do not add leaderboard points. Match points and Tournament bonus picks are scored separately.

8. My Stats & head-to-head

Open My Stats for your season dashboard — or tap any name on the Leaderboard to see someone else’s profile.

Your season at a glance

  • Pool rank and total points (match picks + tournament bonus).
  • Exact scores and pick accuracy — how often you’ve earned points on finished matches.
  • Achievements — badges you unlock as you play (e.g. 🎯 Oracle for 3+ exacts in a matchday, 🎰 Bold Hero for a nailed 2× bold exact, 🤖 AI Slayer for outscoring every bot on a matchday). Hover or tap a badge to read what it means.

Season breakdown & form

  • Match vs tournament points — see where your total comes from.
  • Bold picks — how many you used and how many paid off.
  • Beat all AIs — matches where you scored more than every bot in the pool.
  • Lucky team / Nemesis team — sides you’ve read well (or badly) when they’re involved.
  • Best matchday — your highest-scoring round so far.
  • Recent form — your last five finished picks with points earned (bold picks marked).

Head-to-head (one-on-one)

Scroll to Head-to-head on any player page to settle a rivalry match by match — not who won the real game, but who picked better in your pool.

  • Choose a pool mate from the Compare with dropdown (friends, rivals, or an AI bot).
  • The scoreboard shows each player’s total pool points plus a match wins record — e.g. 12 – 8 means the first player outscored the other on 12 shared finished matches (ties count separately).
  • The table lists every match you both predicted and that has a final result: both scorelines, points earned, and the round winner highlighted.
  • Only finished matches count. If you haven’t shared any yet, pick someone else or check back after more results.
Tip: Use head-to-head before a big matchday — “I’m 9–6 up on Dave” is better trash talk than total leaderboard rank.
